📜 OUR STORY

A Legacy of Excellence
Since 1974

Kalinabiri Secondary School was established in 1974 as a modest community school with just three teachers and sixty students. Named after the serene Kalinabiri hills that overlook Ntinda, Kampala, the institution was founded on an uncompromising belief: that every young person deserves access to world-class education regardless of their background.

Over the decades, Kalinabiri has grown from strength to strength. What began as a small O-Level institution expanded in 1985 to include A-Level streams, adding Science, Humanities, and Commerce combinations. By the year 2000, we had earned a reputation as one of Uganda's most consistent performers in the UNEB examinations — a legacy we have defended and extended every year since.

Today, Kalinabiri Secondary School stands as a beacon of educational excellence, enrolling over 2,500 students from across Uganda and the wider East African region. Our alumni include university professors, medical doctors, engineers, entrepreneurs, leaders in government, and innovators who are shaping Uganda's future. Our story is still being written — and every student who joins us adds a new chapter.

📋 DISCIPLINE

Our Code of Conduct

Clear expectations and standards that maintain a safe, orderly, and nurturing learning environment.

👔 Uniform Policy +
All students are required to wear the official Kalinabiri Secondary School uniform at all times while on campus and during school-sponsored events. The uniform consists of the navy-blue blazer/sweater, white shirt, grey trousers/skirt, and official footwear. Hair styles must be neat and professional. The uniform reflects our values of discipline, pride, and unity. Non-compliance will result in disciplinary action, including denial of entry to class until the matter is addressed with parents/guardians.
📅 Attendance Requirements +
Regular attendance is mandatory for all enrolled students. A minimum of 90% attendance per term is required to sit for examinations. Students must report to school by 7:30 AM for day students and by 6:00 PM on Sunday evenings for boarders. Absences must be reported by a parent/guardian within 24 hours via the official school communication channel. Extended absences require prior written approval from the Headteacher's office. Truancy is treated as a serious offence.
📝 Academic Integrity +
Kalinabiri Secondary School upholds the highest standards of academic honesty. All forms of cheating, plagiarism, or dishonesty in academic work are strictly prohibited. This includes copying homework, using unauthorized materials during tests/examinations, and false representation of work as one's own. Violations will result in a zero grade, parental notification, and disciplinary review. Repeat offences may lead to suspension or permanent expulsion.
📋 Examination Regulations +
All students sitting UNEB or internal examinations must present a valid student ID and adhere to examination rules as stipulated by the examinations council. Students must arrive at the examination room at least 15 minutes before the start time. Electronic devices are strictly prohibited in examination rooms. Bags and personal items will be secured at the front of the room. Any communication between students during an examination, or possession of unauthorized material, constitutes malpractice and will be reported to UNEB.
🛡️ Anti-Bullying Policy +
Kalinabiri maintains a zero-tolerance policy on bullying, harassment, and intimidation in any form — physical, verbal, cyber, or social. Every student has the right to feel safe and respected. Bullied students are encouraged to report incidents to any teacher, prefect, or the student welfare office without fear of retaliation. Confidentiality will be maintained throughout the investigation process. Perpetrators face suspension, counselling, and in severe cases, permanent expulsion.
💻 ICT Usage Policy +
Students are permitted to use school computers, tablets, and the internet solely for educational purposes. Accessing inappropriate content, engaging in online harassment, downloading unauthorized software, or attempting to bypass school network security will result in immediate revocation of ICT privileges and further disciplinary action. All students must sign the ICT Acceptable Use Agreement before accessing school digital resources. The school reserves the right to monitor all network activity.
